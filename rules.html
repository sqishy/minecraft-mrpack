🏁 The basics

Rally is a time-trial racing mode you ride on Pokémon. You lay out a course, mount up, and race for the fastest time. Races can be run solo or multiplayer, and your times are saved to a per-race leaderboard with your personal best.

You enter a race at a Rally Kiosk, which lists the registered races, leaderboards, and record holders. See the Recipes page for how to build one.

🐎 Your team — up to 3 mounts

You bring a team of up to three rideable Pokémon into a race and quick-switch between them on the fly (the mount 2 / mount 3 keys). The switch is seamless — your speed and heading carry straight over to the new mount.

Switching is the core strategy: a benched mount recovers stamina while it rests, so you rotate to a fresh mount before the current one tires out. Different mounts also shine on different terrain.

Which Pokémon can you ride, and how do their ride stats compare? See Rideable Mounts.

📊 Ride stats

Every mount rolls five ride stats (as ranges, a bit like IVs):

  • Speed — top speed. The single biggest factor in race time.
  • Acceleration — how fast you reach top speed (and recover it after slowing).
  • Skill — handling: tighter, more controlled turns.
  • Jump — jump strength, and it feeds into drift boosts.
  • Stamina — how long the mount can run before tiring (see below).

⚡ Stamina & fatigue

Racing drains stamina. Manage it or pay for it:

  • Each time a mount's stamina dips below the fatigue threshold, it picks up a small permanent speed penalty for the rest of the race — run it ragged and it stays slow.
  • If stamina hits zero, the mount crawls at a floor speed until it recovers.
  • Benched mounts recover stamina, so swap out a tired mount before it bottoms out.

Rule of thumb: rotate your three mounts so each is fresh when it's out front, and none ever hits zero.

🌀 Steering & drifting

Turning is momentum-based, not instant. Hold a direction and your turn rate ramps up; the faster you're going, the wider you turn. Higher Skill tightens all of that up.

Drifting is how you take sharp corners and gain speed: hold the drift key while steering to carve a tight turn (at a small speed cost), then release to fire off a speed boost — the longer you held the drift, the bigger the boost. Chaining clean drifts through corners is the main way to shave time.

You can steer with the camera (mouse) or with A/D (WASD mode); controllers are supported too.

🧱 Terrain

The block beneath your mount matters. Every tick the game reads the ground you're on and applies a terrain effect — some surfaces slow you or drain extra stamina, others are neutral or fast. Ice is slick and hurts handling; rough ground (mud, sand, snow) taxes stamina; and there are trap/boost pads built into courses.

Mounts and items can specialize for terrain: a mount's type ability and its held item can turn a bad surface into a good one.

✨ Abilities & held items

Two systems let you tune a mount for a race:

  • Race abilities — every mount gets a passive ability from each of its types (so a dual-type has two). They do things like scale stats as stamina drops, boost drifts, or grant terrain immunity. Full list on Race Abilities.
  • Held items — give a mount an item and it changes how it races: more speed, more stamina, stronger drifts, terrain perks, or clutch once-per-race saves. Full list on Held Items.

🛠️ Setting up a race

Building a course uses the Rally blocks and items (craft them — see Recipes):

  • Place a Rally Start to anchor the track's start/finish.
  • Lay out the route and checkpoints with Rally Flags.
  • Optionally mark lanes and the course with Race Line blocks.
  • Put down a Rally Kiosk so players can register/enter the race and view leaderboards.
  • Players join by interacting with Racing Reins at the kiosk.

This guide is a plain-English summary of the Rally systems — exact tuning values live in the mod config.
